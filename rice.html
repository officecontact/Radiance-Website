Basmati Rice

GI-tagged long-grain aromatic rice from Himalayan foothills — the world's most prized rice variety. Grain length ≥6.61mm, elongation ≥1.7x, aroma index 6+.

APEDA RCMCIndia OrganicEU OrganicGI Tagged

Basmati rice is produced exclusively in the Himalayan terai belt of Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, and Uttarakhand. Each variety is processed to APEDA's Basmati Rice (Quality and Export) Regulations. Indian Rice Export — Frequently Asked Questions

What types of rice does India export?+
India exports Basmati rice (1121, Pusa, 1718, 1509, Traditional) and Non-Basmati rice (IR64 Parboiled, Sona Masoori, Swarna MTU 7029, PR11/PR14, Ponni, Gobindobhog). Radiance Overseas exports all 12 varieties with APEDA certification and EU Organic compliance.
Is Radiance Overseas an APEDA registered rice exporter?+
Yes. Radiance Overseas holds a valid APEDA (Agricultural & Processed Food Products Export Development Authority) registration, which is mandatory for all rice exporters from India. We are also certified for EU Organic and India Organic (NPOP) rice.
What is the MOQ for basmati rice export from India?+
MOQ for Basmati rice starts at 25 MT per variety. Non-Basmati rice: 50 MT. We accommodate smaller trial orders (5–10 MT) for new buyers at slight premium. Custom processing (raw/sella/steamed, broken %, moisture %) is available.
What packaging is used for Indian rice exports?+
Standard: 25 kg PP woven bags with inner HDPE liner, 50 kg jute bags, and 1 kg/2 kg/5 kg retail consumer packs. Bulk container shipments (FCL 20'/40') also available. All packaging is food-grade and complies with EU EC 1935/2004 and US FDA regulations.
Which countries does Radiance Overseas export Indian rice to?+
We export to Saudi Arabia, UAE, Iraq, Kuwait, Iran, Nigeria, Kenya, UK, Germany, Netherlands, USA, Canada, Australia, Malaysia, and Singapore. We are experienced with GCC SASO, EU TRACES system, US FDA registration, and African phytosanitary requirements.
What is the difference between Basmati and Non-Basmati rice for export?+
Basmati rice (GI-tagged to specific Himalayan foothills regions) has long grains (≥6.61mm raw), distinctive aroma, and high elongation ratio (≥1.7). It commands premium prices. Non-Basmati covers all other Indian rice varieties — typically shorter/medium grain, wider cultivation, and more competitive pricing for volume markets in Africa, Middle East, and Southeast Asia.
